Make it work

A few things that matter more than the menu

Food is the raw material, not the trigger

Muscle grows when you make it work harder than last time. Pair this with progressive resistance three days a week, push-ups, squats, bands or dumbbells, or the plan mostly maintains you.

Weigh in weekly, same time

Gaining faster than 0.25–0.3 kg a week is mostly fat, so ease off the add-ons. Flat for 2–3 weeks means add about 200 kcal.

Curd becomes chaas, and only at lunch

Since milk doesn't sit well and curd sours to kapha at night, your only dairy is buttermilk at midday, with roasted jeera. Whey goes in water, never with fruit.

Stock for the week

Paneer and a little curd for chaas, moong (dal and to sprout), chana, besan and sattu, rajma and masoor, ragi flour, and a jar of mixed nuts and seeds kept soaking.

To hit the ~2,600 kcal gaining surplus: add one of these, a katori of rice at lunch, an extra roti, a spoon of ghee, or a second glass of chaas at midday. Small and steady beats a big jump you'd only have to cut later.
